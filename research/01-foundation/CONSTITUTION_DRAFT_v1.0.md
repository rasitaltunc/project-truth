## PREAMBLE

We, the investigators, researchers, community members, and citizens committed to evidence-based understanding of complex networks, do establish this Constitution for Project Truth.

We recognize that:

**Evidence is the foundation of truth.** Isolated facts are noise. Networks of facts are signal. Our mission is to preserve, organize, and illuminate these networks so that patterns become visible, responsibility becomes clear, and truth becomes difficult to deny.

**Sources are sacred.** Investigators who expose corruption, crime, and abuse risk their safety, their careers, and sometimes their lives. We commit to absolute protection of investigative sources as a matter of conscience and law.

**Community intelligence is powerful.** No single person sees all the connections. The wisdom of many investigators—drawing from different fields, different countries, different lived experiences—creates insight that no algorithm alone could generate. We commit to genuine community participation in deciding what matters.

**Technology must be transparent.** Algorithms and AI can amplify bias or fabricate patterns as easily as they reveal truth. We commit to explaining our tools, acknowledging their limitations, and never hiding behind "the algorithm decided."

**Principles matter more than rules.** Rules are guides; principles are commitments. When rules and principles conflict, principles prevail. When circumstances are unclear, we appeal to principles, not legal hairsplitting.

To protect these commitments, we adopt this Constitution and pledge to govern by it.

## ARTICLE I: CORE PRINCIPLES (Immutable)

### PRINCIPLE 1: EVIDENCE INTEGRITY

**Statement:**
All evidence in Truth's networks is preserved, never fabricated, altered, or destroyed. Evidence can be contextualized, annotated, quality-tiered, or removed due to serious harm—but never changed. The integrity of the record is sacred.

**What This Means:**
- Original documents are archived exactly as received
- No retroactive editing of evidence
- Context is added as annotations, not embedded in original
- Quality tiering (unverified / community-voted / peer-reviewed) is transparent
- Removal (in extreme cases) is logged and auditable

**What This Prevents:**
- AI that "corrects" evidence
- Deletion to protect powerful people
- Retroactive sanitization of damaging connections
- Manipulation of timestamps or metadata

### PRINCIPLE 2: SOURCE PROTECTION

**Statement:**
Investigators who provide sources are protected absolutely. Source identity is never revealed, logged, or contextually guessable. Protection is architectural (built into code), not just policy (easy to violate).

**What This Means:**
- IP addresses are never logged for anonymous submissions
- Source metadata is encrypted end-to-end
- Patterns of submission cannot reveal identity
- Even subpoena cannot compel what the system doesn't collect
- Source protection survives government requests, corporate pressure, or platform compromise

**What This Prevents:**
- Government identifying journalists' sources
- Harassment of truth-tellers
- Chilling effect on investigation
- Reverse-engineering of identity from metadata

### PRINCIPLE 3: COMMUNITY INTELLIGENCE

**Statement:**
Investigation networks are built collectively. The wisdom of many investigators—with different expertise, different networks, different life experiences—shapes which connections are explored, which patterns are recognized, which evidence is trusted.

**What This Means:**
- Community votes on which investigations to prioritize
- Community suggests new connections
- Community can challenge peer-reviewed evidence
- The most active investigators don't get veto power
- Newcomers can contribute; contribution history determines influence (not name recognition)

**What This Prevents:**
- Editorial gatekeeping
- "Famous investigator" dominating conversations
- Majority tyranny (one perspective silencing others)
- Concentration of power

### PRINCIPLE 4: ALGORITHMIC TRANSPARENCY

**Statement:**
Our tools, recommendations, and decision-making processes are understandable. We explain our choices. We document our mistakes. We never hide complexity behind "the algorithm did it."

**What This Means:**
- Release technical details on a 6-month delay (prevents gaming; allows auditing)
- Real-time explanation of why evidence was flagged/removed
- Quarterly audit of algorithmic bias
- Open discussion of how recommendations are generated
- Acknowledgment of errors and commitment to fixing them

**What This Prevents:**
- "Trust us; we're good"
- Undisclosed bias in recommendations
- Algorithmic accountability gaps
- Gaming the algorithm (exploiting hidden rules)

### PRINCIPLE 5: ADAPTIVE GOVERNANCE

**Statement:**
Governance rules exist to serve principles, not vice versa. As we learn, grow, and face unforeseen challenges, we adapt operational rules while protecting core principles. The constitution evolves; the commitments remain.

**What This Means:**
- Rules can be changed via amendment process
- Principles cannot be changed (require consensus + sustained commitment)
- We regularly revisit rules to see if they're working
- Honest mistakes trigger learning, not punishment
- Community input shapes evolution

**What This Prevents:**
- Ossified governance that ignores experience
- Creeping violation of principles (gradual rule changes that undermine commitments)
- Governance-as-excuse ("we have to follow the rule even though it's clearly wrong")

## ARTICLE II: OPERATIONAL RULES

### RULE 1: SEALED DOCUMENTS

**Definition:**
A sealed document is evidence marked as restricted by its original investigator. It remains inaccessible to the public but accessible to Truth's operational teams and authorized researchers.

**Unsealing Conditions:**
A sealed document can be unsealed ONLY by:

1. **Original Investigator** — Can unseal at any time, for any reason
2. **Court Order** — Can unseal only via:
   - Court order from court of competent jurisdiction
   - Truth legal team files objection (buys time; doesn't prevent unsealing)
   - Truth board can appeal on constitutional grounds (delays further)
3. **Truth Board + Community Veto** — Can unseal in cases of imminent serious harm:
   - Requires unanimous board agreement
   - 72-hour community veto period
   - If 2/3 of active community objects, unsealing is blocked
   - Abuse of this power triggers constitutional amendment

**Special Cases:**
- Sealed documents are never visible in network view (even anonymized)
- Sealed documents are never included in AI analysis (to prevent reverse-engineering)
- Sealed documents count toward connection statistics but not visible connections
- In rare cases (imminent serious harm), specific facts can be extracted without revealing source

**Violations:**
- Unsealing without authorization: permanent ban + legal action
- Revealing sealed document content: permanent ban + legal action
- Attempting to guess sealed document contents: first offense = warning; subsequent = ban

---

### RULE 2: SOURCE ANONYMITY

**Technical Requirements:**
- IP addresses are never logged (eliminated at server architecture level)
- Submission metadata (timestamps, browser, location) is minimized
- Anonymous submissions use Tor/VPN detection but don't block (privacy > verification)
- No correlation analysis linking multiple submissions to probable same source

**Legal Commitment:**
- Truth will not voluntarily disclose anonymous source identity
- Truth will fight subpoenas for source identity
- Truth maintains cryptographic proof of "we can't provide what we don't have"
- Violating this commitment triggers constitutional amendment

**Operational Implications:**
- Investigators cannot see their own submission metadata (prevents them from accidentally revealing themselves)
- Appeals cannot reference "this submission came from person X" (because we don't track that)
- Source verification happens via evidence quality, not identity

---

### RULE 3: EVIDENCE QUALITY TIERS

**Tier 1: Peer-Reviewed Evidence**
- Reviewed by 2+ experts in domain
- Sources are cited and verified
- Methodology is explained and defensible
- Badge: Green checkmark + "PEER-REVIEWED"
- Visibility: Highest ranking in search; featured in networks

**Tier 2: Community-Voted Evidence**
- Submitted with sources and reasoning
- Community votes on credibility (investigators and verified users only)
- Requires 50%+ approval from ≥20 voters
- Badge: Blue circle + "COMMUNITY-VERIFIED"
- Visibility: Standard ranking; appears in networks

**Tier 3: Unverified Evidence**
- Raw submission; no verification yet
- Clearly labeled "UNVERIFIED"
- Visible but lower ranking
- No badge; gray indicator
- Visibility: Lower ranking; visible only in detailed network view
- Can be promoted to Tier 2 via community voting

**Tier 4: Disputed Evidence**
- Community consensus says "this is likely false"
- Label: Red X + "COMMUNITY DISPUTED"
- Stays visible (for historical context)
- Can be appealed (see Article IV)

**Tier 5: Removed Evidence**
- Imminent serious harm + no legitimate public interest
- Preserved in encrypted archive (auditable but not public)
- Decision is logged and reviewable
- Can be restored via appeals process

---

### RULE 4: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**Who Can File:**
- Persons directly mentioned in evidence
- Investigators who created conflicting evidence
- Community members (if they believe false accusation harms public interest)

**Process:**
1. **Filing** (1 day)
   - Claimant files detailed dispute
   - Must cite specific evidence + specific claim
   - Must state why claim is false/misleading

2. **Automated Response** (1 day)
   - System confirms filing
   - Evidence is labeled "DISPUTED — Under Review"
   - Original investigator gets notification (72 hours to respond)

3. **Investigation** (2 weeks)
   - Dispute panel reviews both sides
   - Independent research if needed
   - Interviews with investigators (optional)

4. **Decision** (1 week)
   - Panel decides: True / Contested / False / Misleading
   - Decision is logged with full reasoning
   - Claimant + investigator both notified

5. **Implementation**
   - If "False": Evidence moved to Tier 4 (disputed) or Tier 5 (removed)
   - If "Contested": Both narratives presented; community decides
   - If "True": Dismissal; complaint flagged to prevent harassment

6. **Appeal** (ongoing)
   - Either party can appeal to Constitutional Appeals Panel
   - Appeal cites constitutional principles, not just factual disagreement
   - Panel makes final decision

---

### RULE 5: AMENDMENT PROCESS

**Proposal Phase (2 weeks):**
- Any community member proposes amendment
- Requires 10 co-signers (prevents frivolous proposals)
- Public posting period; community discussion
- Proposer responds to questions

**Drafting Phase (4 weeks):**
- Amendment committee (7 community members) drafts language
- Iterative feedback from community + board
- Philosophy section is written (explaining why)
- Legal review ensures no constitutional conflicts

**Ratification Phase (6 weeks):**
- Requires BOTH:
  - 2/3 board vote (stability)
  - 50% community vote (legitimacy)
- If board + community disagree:
  - Passes automatically after 6 months if both still disagree
  - Acknowledges that legitimacy takes time

**Constitutional Locks:**
- The 5 Core Principles cannot be amended
- Source protection mechanisms cannot be weakened (can only be strengthened)
- Public access to evidence cannot be eliminated

**Amendment Precedent:**
- All amendments create precedent for future interpretation
- Philosophy booklet is updated with each amendment

## ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURES

### THE COMMUNITY COUNCIL

**Composition:**
- 100-300 active contributors (investigators, moderators, researchers)
- Divided into working groups: Evidence, Policy, Technology, Community, Ethics
- Elected representatives (rotating, 6-month terms)

**Authority:**
- Votes on major amendments
- Elects board members (1-2 per year)
- Can initiate investigations (if 10+ members agree)
- Can override board decisions via 2/3 majority (with 72-hour delay for reconsideration)

**How to Join:**
- 3+ verified contributions + 90 days active participation
- Application to working group
- Vote by existing members (50%+ approval)

### THE TRUTH BOARD

**Composition:**
- 5-7 members (odd number to prevent ties)
- Includes: founders, community-elected representative, independent ethics expert
- 2-3 year staggered terms (prevents total turnover)

**Authority:**
- Day-to-day operational decisions
- Hire/fire staff
- Major feature decisions (with community input)
- Emergency override (with limits; see below)

**How to Join:**
- Community elections (every 18 months)
- Board self-selects independent experts
- Founders remain (with board veto)
- Term limits: Max 3 consecutive terms (6-9 years)

### THE APPEALS PANEL

**Composition:**
- 3 rotating members from community council
- Serve 6-month rotation (constant turnover)
- Cannot include board members or staff

**Authority:**
- Hears appeals of board decisions
- Makes binding rulings in 95% of cases
- Edge cases escalate to constitutional amendment
- Decisions become precedent (documented in philosophy booklet)

**Process:**
- 2-week review period
- Hearing with both parties present (video call)
- Decision within 1 week
- Full reasoning documented

### THE PHILOSOPHY MAINTAINERS

**Composition:**
- 3-5 community members
- Rotate annually (prevent elite capture)
- Experts in constitutional law + organizational governance

**Authority:**
- Maintain constitution + philosophy booklet
- Clarify (not change) principles
- Document precedents + new cases
- Propose non-binding clarifications

**Not a Decision-Making Body:**
- They clarify language; they don't create new rules
- They document decisions; they don't make them
- All proposals go through normal amendment process

## ARTICLE IV: EMERGENCY OVERRIDE

**When It Can Be Used:**
- Imminent serious harm (not theoretical, not future, not speculative)
- Examples: child abuse material, active hostage situation, credible active threat
- Does NOT include: offensive content, misinformation, legal threats, corporate pressure

**Who Can Use It:**
- Requires unanimous agreement: CEO + Chief Counsel + Community Representative
- Not a single person's decision
- Community Representative must agree (prevents admin overreach)

**Process:**
1. **Immediate Action** (same minute)
   - Content is removed / access is revoked
   - As narrow as possible (only affected content; no broader sweep)

2. **Notification** (within 1 hour)
   - Community Council is notified
   - Explanation is provided
   - Evidence of harm is documented

3. **Investigation** (within 24 hours)
   - Full review of decision
   - Community can file objections

4. **Formal Review** (within 30 days)
   - Appeals panel hears case
   - Considers constitutional amendment if needed
   - Decision becomes precedent

**Abuse Prevention:**
- Using emergency override without genuine imminent harm = grounds for removal
- Community has right to force amendment (reversing override)
- All override decisions are audited annually

## ARTICLE V: DATA PROTECTION & PRIVACY

### Source Data

**What We Collect (Minimally):**
- Content of submission
- Approximate time of submission (day only; not hour/minute)
- Tier level (if verified vs. unverified)

**What We Never Collect:**
- IP addresses
- Device fingerprints
- Location data
- Metadata beyond submission date

**How It's Encrypted:**
- End-to-end encryption for sensitive submissions
- Decryption keys held separately from submission data
- Regular audits by third-party security firm

### Community Member Data

**What We Collect:**
- Nickname / Display name
- Verification level (tier badges)
- Contribution history (pseudonymous)
- Vote history (in disputes; not linked to identity)

**What We Don't Collect:**
- Real names (unless provided voluntarily)
- Email addresses (optional, for notifications only)
- Demographic information
- Location
- Social media profiles

**Retention:**
- All data is deleted if account is closed
- Contribution history is anonymized after 7 years
- Appeals decisions stay (for precedent) but names are redacted

## ARTICLE VI: TRANSPARENCY & ACCOUNTABILITY

### Decision Logs

**Immediate (same day):**
- Decision outcome only
- "Evidence was flagged as unverified"

**24 Hours:**
- Reasoning + principles cited
- "Per Integrity Principle, insufficient sources"

**1 Week:**
- Appeal process + voting breakdown
- "5 votes to flag, 2 to allow, 0 abstain"

**Quarterly Audit:**
- Full reasoning + context
- Accessible to board members
- Annual summary published to community

### Transparency Reports

**Quarterly (4x/year):**
- Decisions made + reasoning
- Disputes filed + outcomes
- Amendments proposed + ratified
- Community participation metrics

**Annual (1x/year):**
- Comprehensive report on governance
- Third-party audit of philosophy booklet
- Amendment status
- Lessons learned

## ARTICLE VII: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY & REUSE

**Our Content Is Open:**
- All evidence is public domain (or Creative Commons)
- Researchers can reuse for academic work
- Press can republish with attribution
- No paywalls; no profit-taking

**Attribution Required:**
- Evidence includes source documentation
- Derivative works must cite original source
- Platform cannot be rebranded

**Limitations:**
- Cannot remove evidence and claim your own
- Cannot use Truth network to create proprietary AI training
- Cannot republish with false attribution

---

## ARTICLE VIII: CONSTITUTIONAL EVOLUTION

### How Interpretation Happens

**Day-to-Day:**
- Board + community make decisions citing the constitution
- Appeals panel clarifies (not changes) meaning
- Philosophy booklet documents precedent

**When Conflict Arises:**
- Community Council votes on interpretation
- Philosophy maintainers document debate
- If no consensus, amendment process is triggered

### How the Constitution Evolves

**Minor Clarification:**
- Philosophy booklet can clarify without amendment
- Requires 2/3 board agreement
- Community has 2-week objection period

**Major Change:**
- Full amendment process required
- Philosophy booklet documents what changed + why

---

## ARTICLE IX: DISSOLUTION & LEGACY

**If Truth Becomes Inactive:**
- All evidence is transferred to public archive (Internet Archive, Wikimedia, or similar)
- Philosophy booklet is published (Creative Commons)
- Source anonymity is preserved even after dissolution
- Community has right to fork + continue under new governance

**If Truth Is Captured:**
- Community can initiate constitutional amendment
- If board refuses 50%+ amendment, community can fork
- Forked project inherits all evidence (in compliance with source protection)
