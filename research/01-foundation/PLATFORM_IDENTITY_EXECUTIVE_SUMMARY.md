## THE CORE INSIGHT: THREE LEGAL LAYERS

Truth must clearly separate into three layers with different legal models:

### Layer 1: Infrastructure (Library Model) — LOW LIABILITY
- Court documents and public records
- Network visualization
- Search tools
- Community-curated relationships (with voting)
- **Liability: Low**
- **Defense: Innocent dissemination (platforms don't republish for truth); Section 230 (community content)**

### Layer 2: Investigation Tools (Research Tools) — MODERATE LIABILITY
- Gap analysis suggestions
- Heat maps and consensus data
- Export functionality (GraphML for researchers)
- Statistical analysis
- **Liability: Moderate**
- **Defense: "We provide research tools; users draw conclusions"**

### Layer 3: AI Analysis (Limited Publisher) — HIGH LIABILITY
- Chat panel responses
- Daily questions generated by AI
- AI-extracted connections from documents
- **Liability: High**
- **Defense: Editorial standards, peer review, error correction mechanism**
- **Safeguards Required: Insurance, confidence thresholds, source verification**

---

## CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS (Non-Negotiable)

If Truth adopts the public infrastructure model, these are required:

### 1. Transparency
- Every connection shows: source document(s), confidence level, who added it
- Users can see full provenance (who said what, when)
- AI-generated content clearly labeled as such

### 2. Error Reporting
- "This is false" button on every node/link
- Public investigation tracker
- Removal within 48 hours of confirmed error
- Corrected explanation posted (transparency)

### 3. Confidence Thresholds
- AI outputs only assert connections with 70%+ confidence
- Lower-confidence items marked "exploratory"
- Chat responses include confidence level
- High-risk claims (criminal accusations) require peer review

### 4. Source Verification
- Every connection traces to original document
- Users can audit reasoning chain
- AI cannot infer beyond what document supports
- Hallucinatory inferences removed by review

### 5. Community Governance
- Peer review system (quarantine model)
- Voting on suggested connections
- Public moderation log
- Appeals process

### 6. Insurance
- $2-3M media liability coverage (minimum)
- Covers defamation, AI liability, privacy claims
- ~$20-30K/year cost

## WHAT THIS PROTECTS AGAINST

### Without Safeguards
- AI extracts false connection from document
- Connection displayed prominently
- User sues for defamation
- Truth has no defense
- **Damages: $500K-$1M+**

### With Safeguards
- Same false connection
- Labeled "confidence: 68%, unverified"
- Error reported immediately
- Removed within 48 hours with correction posted
- Good faith error correction evident
- **Damages if sued: $50-200K (mostly defensible)**

**Difference: Safeguards reduce damages by 75%.**

## WHAT TRUTH CANNOT CLAIM

Truth cannot position as:
- ✗ **Publisher** — "These connections are verified facts" (creates full liability)
- ✗ **Investigative Journalism** — No human journalists, can't claim journalistic privilege
- ✗ **Neutral Platform** — AI curation is not neutral (Section 230 won't apply)
- ✗ **Tool Provider** — Providing curated data, not blank-canvas tool

---

## WHAT TRUTH CAN CLAIM

Truth can position as:
- ✓ **Civic Infrastructure** — Transparency tool for accountability
- ✓ **Research Infrastructure** — Data organized for researchers to investigate
- ✓ **Community Platform** — Community creates connections; platform moderates
- ✓ **Investigative Aid** — Visualizes existing investigations; doesn't conduct investigations

## KEY LEGAL PRECEDENTS

### 1. Innocent Dissemination (Libraries Not Liable)
- **Case:** Vizetelly v. Mudie's Select Library (UK)
- **Holding:** Circulating library not liable for defamatory books
- **Principle:** Distributors ≠ publishers unless they endorse content
- **Application:** Truth curates, doesn't endorse → lower liability

### 2. ISP Immunity (Passive Hosting)
- **Case:** Cubby v. CompuServe (1991)
- **Holding:** ISP with "no more editorial control than library" is not liable
- **Principle:** Passive distribution differs from active curation
- **Application:** Truth's infrastructure layer is passive; AI layer is active

### 3. Platform Immunity (Section 230)
- **Case:** Wikipedia v. Defamation Claimant (Florida 2021)
- **Holding:** Section 230 bars defamation claim against platform for user content
- **Principle:** Users are liable, not platform
- **Application:** Truth's community voting system qualifies for Section 230

### 4. AI-Generated Content (No Immunity)
- **Status:** Unsettled law (no final court ruling)
- **Consensus:** Section 230 does NOT protect AI-generated content
- **Principle:** If platform creates content, platform is liable
- **Application:** Truth's chat and AI extractions are Truth-created, not protected

### 5. Curation ≠ Publishing
- **Case:** TikTok Third Circuit (2024)
- **Status:** Changing landscape (algorithm IS editorial choice?)
- **Principle:** Courts increasingly scrutinizing algorithmic curation
- **Application:** Truth must explain why its curation is neutral/passive
