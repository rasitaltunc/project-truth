## 10-MINUTE READ: CORE PRINCIPLES

**If you read nothing else, understand these:**

### 1. DISPLAY, DON'T CREATE
Project Truth shows evidence. It doesn't make original claims.

❌ Bad: "John Smith engaged in corruption"
✅ Good: "Court alleges John Smith engaged in corruption [link to court document]"

**Why:** Section 230 protection requires we be "platform, not publisher"

---

### 2. VERIFICATION IN TIERS
Each piece of evidence has a confidence level.

```
🟦 OFFICIAL (95%+) — Court document, corporate registry
🟩 JOURNALISTIC (70-90%) — Reported by credentialed journalist
🟨 COMMUNITY (<70%) — User-submitted, unverified
🔴 DISPUTED — Contradictory evidence shown
```

**Why:** Users need to know which evidence is strong vs. weak

---

### 3. TRANSPARENCY ALWAYS WINS
Show your work. Disclose limitations. Allow disputes.

**Transparent methodology > proprietary black box**

This is why Bellingcat succeeds and Palantir Gotham fails.

---

### 4. THREE-TIER MODERATION
- **Automatic:** Obvious abuse (spam, PII)
- **Community:** User flags + triage
- **Human:** Expert review + appeals

**Why:** Fair decisions build trust; documented decisions defend in court

---

### 5. LEGAL IS NOT OPTIONAL
You need:
- Corporation (Section 230 protection)
- Lawyer (on retainer)
- Insurance ($20K/year)

**Why:** One defamation lawsuit without these = bankruptcy

---

### 6. GDPR/KVKK MATTERS
If you serve EU or Turkish users, you must comply.

- EU: Fines up to 6% of revenue
- Turkey: Fines + platform access blocking

**Why:** You can ignore it, but at existential risk

---

### 7. DATA MINIMIZATION
Only collect what's necessary.

✅ Collect: Names, positions, relationships
❌ Collect: Phone numbers, home addresses, SSNs, photos (without consent)

**Why:** More data = higher privacy risk + higher liability

---

### 8. RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE
If you find a crime:

- CSAM → Report to NCMEC (mandatory)
- Active threat → Report to police (strongly recommended)
- Past corruption → Publish (that's journalism)

**Why:** Some reporting is legally required; all should be ethically sound
