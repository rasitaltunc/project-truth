KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS
================================================================================

CRITICAL FINDING #1: Gamification DECREASES Accuracy in High-Stakes Domains
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Source: Prestwood et al. (2015), Zooniverse animal classification study
Finding: Speed rewards → 28% accuracy DROP (72% → 96% without rewards)
Mechanism: Extrinsic motivation triggers System 1 (fast/auto) not System 2 (slow/deliberate)
Implication for Truth: AVOID leaderboards, speed rewards, point accumulation

CRITICAL FINDING #2: Staking Makes People Careful (Asymmetric Payoff)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Source: Stack Overflow (reputation at risk), Polymarket (money at stake)
Finding: Higher stake = higher accuracy (Polymarket: +20% with 10x money)
Mechanism: Loss aversion + asymmetric payoff (lose 100, gain 30)
Implication for Truth: IMPLEMENT reputation staking with tier-based scaling

CRITICAL FINDING #3: Discovery Moments Drive Intrinsic Motivation
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Source: Zooniverse ("you're the first"), FromThePage (volunteer transcription)
Finding: "You discovered this first" = +38% engagement, 88% retention
Mechanism: Flow state (optimal challenge) + meaningful contribution
Implication for Truth: ADD discovery celebrations, impact visibility

CRITICAL FINDING #4: Civic Duty Scales (Mission-Driven Systems Work)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Source: Wikipedia (280K+ volunteers, $0), OpenStreetMap (8M+ mappers)
Finding: Mission > money. Intrinsic > extrinsic for volunteer work
Mechanism: "Warm glow" of helping (Andreoni, 1990)
Implication for Truth: BUILD on civic duty foundation, not entertainment gamification

CRITICAL FINDING #5: Bridging Consensus Prevents Sybil Attacks
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Source: Community Notes (X/Twitter), matrix factorization algorithms
Finding: Require approval from DIFFERENT viewpoints, not just N people
Mechanism: Multi-signal validation (geography, tier, expertise)
Implication for Truth: REQUIRE geographic + tier diversity for approval

THE 4-PILLAR ARCHITECTURE (RECOMMENDED DESIGN)
================================================================================

        ┌──────────────────────────────────┐
        │  CIVIC DUTY (Foundation)         │  Why: "This matters"
        │  "You're fighting for truth"     │  No points, no money
        ├──────────────────────────────────┤
        │  STAKING (Engine)                │  How: "You lose if wrong"
        │  Tier-based reputation at risk   │  Asymmetric payoff formula
        ├──────────────────────────────────┤
        │  DISCOVERY (Reward)              │  What: "You found this first!"
        │  Keşif anları (celebration)      │  4 types (first query, new link, etc.)
        ├──────────────────────────────────┤
        │  IMPACT VISIBILITY (Proof)       │  Result: "You prevented 31 false items"
        │  Dashboard showing real impact   │  Concrete metrics, not abstract points
        └──────────────────────────────────┘
                        ↓
                ACCURACY-FIRST
          (High accuracy > retention)

WHAT NOT TO DO:
  ❌ Leaderboards (gaming, competition)
  ❌ Speed rewards (rushing, System 1)
  ❌ Point accumulation (point farming)
  ❌ Social sharing (ego-driven, toxic)
